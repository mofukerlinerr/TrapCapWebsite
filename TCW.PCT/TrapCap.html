How it works

Four simple steps to a reusable wasp trap.

1

Choose a used bottle

Any clean 600–750ml soda bottle becomes the trap body. This turns waste into a practical tool.

2

Screw on TrapCap

Our 3D-printed cap locks onto the bottle and keeps bait secure while letting wasps enter through small holes.

3

Add sweet bait

Fill the trap with sugar water or a non-toxic liquid attractant. Wasps enter, bees stay out, and the trap does the rest.

4

Empty and reuse

Dispose of the contents responsibly, rinse the bottle and repeat. The cap lasts for many cycles and reduces repeated plastic waste.

Built for rangatiratanga (local stewardship)

TrapCap helps communities take local control of invasive wasps, protect native biodiversity, and reduce dependence on imported, single-use traps.

  • Many retail traps are single-use and manufactured overseas.
  • Disposable domes increase landfill waste and need frequent replacement.
  • Chemical sprays can be costly and harmful to non-target native insects.
