The wasp problem

New Zealand is facing one of the highest wasp densities in the world.

Invasive wasps damage crops, injure people, and hurt beekeeping. Most existing products are expensive, harmful to the environment, or single-use. TrapCap offers a sustainable local solution that keeps useful insects safe and reuses plastic bottles already in your home.

How it works

Four simple steps to a reusable wasp trap.

1

Choose a used bottle

Any empty soda bottle becomes the trap body. This turns waste into a practical tool and keeps it from becoming polutions for a while longer.

2

Screw on TrapCap

Our 3D-printed cap locks onto the bottle and keeps bait secure while letting wasps enter through small holes.

3

Add sweet bait

Fill the trap with sugar water or a non-toxic liquid attractant. Wasps enter, bees stay out, and the trap does the rest.

4

Empty and reuse

Dispose of the contents responsibly, rinse the bottle and repeat. The cap lasts for many cycles and reduces repeated plastic waste.
